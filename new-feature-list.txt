 1. The "Waste-to-Negative" Feedback Loop
  While you have a searchTermIntelligence agent, it likely looks at Ads data alone. This agent would close the loop by looking at the CRM "Not Interested" reasons.
   * The Problem: You are bidding on "Ceramic Coating," but a specific search term like "cheap ceramic coating" is driving leads that the sales team marks as notinterested with the reason too_expensive.
   * The Agent: It compares wp_get_not_interested_reasons against ads_get_search_terms.
   * The Output: A list of suggested negative keywords based on actual sales rejection data, not just low CTR.

  2. The "Profitability Oracle" (True ROAS)
  Most Ads managers optimize for Cost Per Lead (CPL). This agent optimizes for Actual Profit.
   * The Problem: Campaign A generates leads at $50 each, and Campaign B at $100. Campaign A looks better. However, CRM data shows Campaign B leads result in $3,000 jobs, while Campaign A leads are $500 "maintenance" jobs.
   * The Agent: It joins ads_get_campaign_performance with wp_get_enquiry_details (specifically the final_value and enquiry_status = 'completed').
   * The Output: A "True ROAS" report that tells you to shift budget from the "high-volume/low-value" campaign to the "low-volume/high-value" one.

  3. The "Post-Click Leak" Detective
  This agent focuses on technical and behavioral friction between the click and the lead.
   * The Problem: You have high spend on a campaign, but the CRM shows zero leads for it, even though GA4 shows high traffic.
   * The Agent: It compares ads_get_campaign_performance (clicks) vs. ga4_get_landing_page_performance (bounce rate) vs. wp_get_enquiries (lead count).
   * The Output: It identifies "Ghost Traffic" (high clicks, no GA4 session—suggesting a slow site) or "Friction Points" (high sessions, high bounce, no leads—suggesting the landing page form is broken or the message doesn't match the
     ad).

  4. The "Local Dominance" Strategist
  Since ceramic coating is a physical service, geography is your most important filter.
   * The Problem: You are spending equally across a 50km radius, but your CRM shows that people more than 20km away convert 80% less because they don't want to drive to the shop.
   * The Agent: It extracts the location/postcode from wp_get_enquiries and compares it to Google Ads "Location" performance (if available via MCP) or GA4 geo-data.
   * The Output: A recommendation to geofence your ads more tightly around high-converting postcodes or to increase bids in wealthy suburbs that show high final_value in the CRM.

  Why these are "Core" to a new project:
  These agents move the project from a Reporting Tool (telling you what happened) to a Business Engine (telling you how to make more money). They leverage the WordPress CRM as the "Source of Truth" for value, which is something most
  third-party Ads tools can't do.
